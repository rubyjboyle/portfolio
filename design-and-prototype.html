Course Description

Introduction to user-centered design and prototyping. Focused on practical methods for interaction design. Topics include the nature of design and the challenges to creating and evaluating good design, as well as specific skills for designing interactive systems.

Course Reflection

The three key things I learned this quarter are:

  • I learned how to get out of my comfort zone to do contextual inquiry in an environment that is professional. Choosing to do an allergy and asthma doctorâ€™s office required an extensive approval that took some time but overall was worth it in the end when I got to interview three medical assistants.
  • I learned how to write a full interview. Understand what leading, bias, and double-barreled questions are and how they can be detrimental to your interview. Also, that the order that you ask questions is just as important as the questions themselves.
  • I also learned how to analyze the data that I created and gathered. At the beginning of this class, I had no idea how an observation or an interview could turn into tangible data by analysis and models.

The three things I want to continue to develop:

  • After this class I want to continue to practice my interviewing skills. I found this to be the most enjoyable part of this class. Even though it was intimidating, I loved talking in person with the medical assistants who were more than happy to give me thirty minutes of their day.
  • I also want to continue to practice observing. I think this process is very interesting and really helps to slow down to really take in what is going on around you.
  • Lastly I also want to know how to work with a researcher in the future to understand what has led to a recommendation for a design decision or direction
