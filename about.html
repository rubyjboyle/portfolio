hello, again.

I'm Ruby Boyle, a recent graduate of UC Irvine, where I pursued studies in Human Computer Interaction and Design. Additionally, I'm currently an UX Accessibility Engineer at shop.com, an e-commerce platform.

I am passionate about the intersection of design and development and have had four years crafting these skills as a front-end developer. With my graduate program I focused on research applications of HCI within healthcare software.

When I am not working, I enjoy getting outside, reading, and cooking.

design philosophy

I adopt an accessibility-first approach when it comes to design and development. This means prioritizing accessibility from the outset, resulting in an enhanced experience for all users. This approach not only benefits the end-users but also proves to be more advantageous for businesses, as opposed to retrofitting accessibility features at the end of the development or design process.

In my personal design methodology, I begin by considering the semantic HTML structure as the foundation. I align this structure with accessibility standards, ensuring that the design closely mirrors what can be feasibly implemented with code.
